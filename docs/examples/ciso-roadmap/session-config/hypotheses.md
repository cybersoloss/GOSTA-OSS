# Session Hypotheses: CISO Priorities 2026

## H-1: Governance-first vs. Identity-first Tension
**Hypothesis:** Multi-domain deliberation will surface a fundamental tension between regulatory domain (NIS2 Article 20 requires governance-first) and threat domain (credential abuse as #1 attack vector requires identity-first), producing a more nuanced sequencing than either domain alone would recommend.
**Test:** Does the deliberation identify this specific tension and produce a resolution that neither single-domain analysis would reach?

## H-2: Execution Bandwidth as Priority Killer
**Hypothesis:** When operational capacity constraints (3-4 initiatives/year) are applied to the combined priority list from all 6 domains, at least 50% of commonly-recommended priorities will be deferred to Year 2+, fundamentally changing what "priority" means.
**Test:** Does the final sequencing differ from generic lists primarily due to execution bandwidth constraints rather than risk ranking?

## H-3: Recovery vs. Prevention Budget Competition
**Hypothesis:** The threat landscape domain (recovery-inclusive defense) and the technology domain (identity hardening, patching) will compete for the same limited budget, producing a tension that generic analyses resolve by simply listing both without acknowledging the trade-off.
**Test:** Does the deliberation explicitly identify budget competition between prevention and recovery investments?

## H-4: Supply Chain Bidirectionality as Non-Obvious Insight
**Hypothesis:** The supply chain domain's "bidirectional position" concept (the organization is both supplier and consumer) will produce a priority reordering that generic analyses miss — specifically, that demonstrating security to customers is a revenue-protection priority, not just a compliance one.
**Test:** Does the final assessment include customer-facing security posture as a business priority rather than purely a compliance item?

## H-5: Cross-Compliance Efficiency as Force Multiplier
**Hypothesis:** The regulatory domain's cross-compliance efficiency concept will identify that building a single control framework covering NIS2 + GDPR + DORA reduces total effort by 40-60% compared to sequential regulation-by-regulation compliance — a structural efficiency invisible to single-regulation analysis.
**Test:** Does the deliberation identify and quantify cross-compliance efficiency as a strategic advantage?
