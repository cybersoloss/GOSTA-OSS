DELIB-001 produced a synthesis report with 4 consensus features (F-03, F-04, F-08 for Q2; F-05 for Q3 post-F-08) and 4 Governor decisions (F-01 staffing, F-02 timing, F-06/F-10 pairing, F-12 investment). The synthesis identified Q3 capacity overflow — not all proposed Q3 features fit within G-3 constraints.

## Decisions

### DECISION-1: F-01 (EU Data Residency) → Option A (modified)
**Choice:** Build F-01 in Q2 with infrastructure team. Assign 2 infrastructure engineers (separate from product engineering FTE pool) so F-01 does not consume G-3 product budget.

**Rationale:** The €400K ARR pipeline is concrete, the regulatory foundation argument from REG-1 is sound, and the infrastructure team exists (currently on maintenance). Treating F-01 as infrastructure investment rather than product feature preserves G-3 budget for product features.

**Impact on G-3:** F-01 effort tracked separately. Product G-3 budget remains 24 FTE-weeks across 2 quarters.

### DECISION-2: F-02 (Automated DSAR Pipeline) → Option A
**Choice:** Build F-02 in Q3. Conduct manual DSAR process capacity audit in Q2 (ACT assigned to Legal Ops).

**Rationale:** Manual process has not failed a deadline yet. Q2 audit confirms capacity. If audit reveals risk, escalate F-02 to Q2 via emergency scope amendment. Preserves Q2 for consensus features.

**Contingency:** If manual process audit reveals capacity gap (<20% margin on 30-day SLA), F-02 escalates to immediate start with Q2 consensus features deprioritized to accommodate.

### DECISION-3: F-06 + F-10 → Option B (F-10 in Q3, F-06 deferred to Q4)
**Choice:** Build F-10 (SSO/SAML) in Q3 with identity refactor Phase 0. Defer F-06 (RBAC) to Q4.

**Rationale:** SSO is a competitive parity gap with 3 documented deal losses — defensive necessity. RBAC has activation distance risk (UX-1's G-2 concern) that requires design work before build. Deferring RBAC to Q4 allows time for proper design specification with zero-config defaults. Identity refactor in Q3 serves F-10 and prepares ground for F-06 in Q4.

**G-2 enforcement:** F-06 specification must include activation distance regression test and zero-config defaults before development begins in Q4. This is a hard precondition.

**G-4 recovery:** Deferring F-06 reduces Q3 Enterprise concentration. Adding F-07 (Usage Analytics, all-segment benefit) to Q3 further rebalances.

### DECISION-4: F-12 (Real-Time Collaboration) → Option A (POC in Q3)
**Choice:** 2-3 week proof-of-concept in Q3. POC scope: basic CRDT text editing with 2 concurrent users. POC exit criteria: validated CRDT library choice, measured latency, estimated full-build effort with ±20% confidence.

**Rationale:** F-12 has the highest market and UX potential but the lowest technical feasibility. A small POC investment (2-3 weeks) produces information that derisk a Q4 commitment. Not investing means making a Q4 build/no-build decision with no data.
