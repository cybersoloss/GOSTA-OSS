# Session Constraints: Vendor-Product Continuity Assessment

## Hard Constraints
1. **Vendor-agnostic analytical frame.** The assessment determines dependency risk from an existing customer perspective. It must NOT produce procurement recommendations, competitive comparisons, migration plans, or vendor advocacy. The AFC Prohibited Frame (G-1) is the authoritative boundary.
2. **Six-signal coverage.** All six vendor viability signals must receive substantive, evidence-grounded assessment in the final deliverable. No signal may be omitted or reduced to a placeholder. The six signals (revenue model exposure, domain depth vs. feature breadth, financial sustainability/consolidation, exit strategy provisions, regulatory embedding depth, vendor stickiness vs. viability) are the minimum analytical scope.
3. **Evidence-grounded scoring.** Every viability score must cite specific evidence item IDs. No domain score shall rest on domain model logic, analyst intuition, or general industry knowledge alone. Scores without evidence citations are invalid.
4. **Dual-level assessment.** Vendor viability and product viability must be scored independently. A healthy vendor may have a declining product; a strong product may sit inside a struggling vendor. Conflating the two levels is a structural error.
5. **Single-session scope.** The assessment is designed to complete within one session. Evidence collection, deliberation, and synthesis are sequential phases within a single finite scope. No ongoing monitoring, periodic reassessment, or multi-session tracking.

## Soft Constraints
1. **Temporal relevance.** Evidence and scoring should reflect the current planning cycle. Historical data is context, not evidence — scores should weight recent signals (last 12 months) more heavily than older indicators unless trend analysis requires longer windows.
2. **Actionable recommendations.** The risk determination should produce mitigation postures that a dependent organization can act on within its normal planning and budget cycles. Abstract risk ratings without actionable postures fail the usefulness test.

## Scope Exclusions
- **Migration planning.** The assessment does not produce migration timelines, alternative vendor evaluations, or transition architectures. These belong to a separate scope triggered by the risk determination.
- **Vendor comparison.** No competitive benchmarking, feature comparison matrices, or market positioning analysis. The assessment is about this vendor and this product in isolation.
- **Procurement advisory.** No buy/renew/cancel recommendations. The assessment surfaces risk; the procurement decision is a separate organizational process.
- **Implementation guidance.** No technical integration advice, deployment recommendations, or configuration guidance for [Target Product].
